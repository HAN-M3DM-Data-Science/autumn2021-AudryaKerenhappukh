Data Problems vs Data Solutions - Unilever AI Recruitment Solution

Audrya Kerenhappukh

27 September, 2021

  • What was the business problem?
  • How was it translated into a data mining problem?
  • What is the realized business value?
  • References

What was the business problem?

Unilever recruits more than 30,000 a people a year and process around 1.8 million job applicants (Marr, n.d.). This process takes a tremendous amount of time and resources if done manually. As finding the right people is one of the most important aspect in business, Unilever cannot afford to overlook talent just because it is buried at the bottom of a pile of CVs that are left unread.

How was it translated into a data mining problem?

To solve the problem mentioned above, Unilever is replacing human recruiters with artificial intelligence system (Booth, 2019). The system is able to scan candidates’ facial expressions, body language and choice of words. It produces an insight score for each candidate to compare a candidate to all of the other candidates.

This solution could be translated into classification task (Provost & Fawcett, 2013). In Unilever case, a scorig model would be able to evaluate each individual candidate and produce a score of how likely each is to be able to be with clients and other people.

What is the realized business value?

With this solution, Unilever is able to look for people with a sense of purpose, systemic thinking, resilience and business acumeen to see whether the would fit in at Unilever. Unilever claimed to have saved 100,000 hours of interviewing time and roughly $1m in recruitment costs each year ( Booth, 2019).
